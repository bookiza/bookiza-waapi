Uranus goes around the Sun every 84 years, and due to its extreme axial tilt (~98°) only one of the poles gets sun light for a full 42 years. The other pole remains in complete darkness until an equinox. There are two places on its orbit where Uranus has the Sun directly above its equator — the point of Equinoxes. The last equinox happened on December 7th, 2007 following which Uranus has its north pole pointing towards the Sun.

Uranus has a thick atmosphere mainly comprising of molecular Hydrogen, Helium and Methane. Lower hydrocarbons like Methane give it an aquamarine or greenish hue. That's why it's called the green planet. The internal structure of Uranus consists of three layers: a solid rocky core, an icy mantle and an outer envelope of liquid hydrogen and helium that extends right up to its atmosphere without any defined boundary/surface.
