It took more than a thousand years and courage to figure out what our Solar System looks like. How it works. Up until the sixteenth century only five planets were known to man. People believed that Earth was at the center of the Universe (see visual) and that Moon, Mercury, Venus, Sun, Mars, Jupiter and Saturn turned around it in succeeding celestial spheres. Strictly in that order.

This was the Geocentric model that ancient astronomer Ptolemy postulated almost 1500 years before Copernicus. Copernicus also put planets in correct order i.e. Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter and Saturn.
